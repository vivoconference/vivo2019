A city famed for it's skyscrapers, for 66 years New York has led the way in building upwards. Whilst it hasn't been home to the tallest building in the world since 1974, there are still many opportunities to get above it all.

30 Rockefeller Plaza

The baby of the observation decks at just 850ft, it is the closest to Weill Cornell Medicine. What it lacks in height, it gains in location, providing great views over Central Park to the North, and the skyscrapers of downtown Manhattan to the South.

Also known as 30 Rock, the building houses the NBC Studios, and a wide range of shopping at ground level - including fashion, gifts, entertainment, and even a LEGO Store.

Look for ticket offers, including a "Sun & Stars" package that allows 2 visits to the observation deck in 24 hours - allowing both day and night viewing of the skyline

Empire State Building

From 1931-1972, it held the crown as the world's tallest building - the longest reign of any skyscraper to date.
